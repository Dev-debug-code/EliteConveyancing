Insurance

It is a condition of your mortgage that you have buildings insurance in place that covers the property for at least its rebuild value. If this is not being arranged by your lender or a management company you must arrange cover yourself from exchange of contracts, as this is when the risk passes to you.

We also recommend that you consider Life Assurance, Critical Illness Insurance and Income Protection Insurance to protect your loved ones and ensure that you can continue to meet your mortgage payments if you become unable to work through illness.

Mortgage Deed

The Mortgage Deed is a legally binding document that gives the lender the right to secure a charge against the property. The property may be repossessed if you fail to meet mortgage repayments.

Your Mortgage Deed must be signed by you and witnessed by an independent adult. Full instructions are in the covering letter to this report.

If an occupier will reside in the property but will not be a joint owner, they must sign either the Occupier section of the Mortgage Deed (if applicable) or a separate Occupier's Waiver form.

Please return the executed Mortgage Deed (and any Occupier's Waiver, if applicable) to us in hard copy.

Mortgage Valuation Report

If your Mortgage Offer contains a Valuation Report you should not rely on it as a survey of the structural adequacy of the property. It is for lender use only and merely gives an indication of value.

We recommend instructing an independent Building Survey by a RICS-accredited surveyor so that any structural or condition-related issues are identified.
